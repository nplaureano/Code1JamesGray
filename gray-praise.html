Critical Praise

"Gray is a generous and sympathetic director of actors" -- A.O. Scott, on Two Lovers for The New York Times

"Gray renders classical cinema even more monumental and painterly than the works at its source" -- Richard Brody, on The Immigrant for The New Yorker

"Gray is more interested in mood and feeling than in the mechanics of plot" -- A.O. Scott, on The Immigrant for The New York Times

"Gray is the most under-appreciated of this country's major filmmakers" -- Ignatiy Vishnevetsky, on The Immigrant for The A.V. CLub

"Gray is committed to the emotional depth of every character" -- David Sims, on The Lost City of Z for The Atlantic

"In Grays films the true creation is neither the effort nor the result: it's the purity of the emotion and the clarity of the idea" -- Richard Brody, on The Lost City of Z for The New Yorker

"Gray fills the screen with intimate reveries and overwhelming spectacle" -- Manohla Dargis, on The Lost City of Z for The New York Times
